Etymology

[ edit ]

Proposed by the 19th-century British psychologist C. Lloyd Morgan.

Proper noun

[ edit ]

Morgan 's canon

  1. ( psychology , zoology ) A fundamental precept of comparative animal psychology , stating that an animal's activity should not be interpreted in terms of higher psychological processes if it can be fairly interpreted in terms of simpler developmental processes.
